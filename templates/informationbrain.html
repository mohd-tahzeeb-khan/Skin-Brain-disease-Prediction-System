Glioma


Symptoms

● Headaches: Persistent and severe headaches are common symptoms of glioma.
● Seizures: Gliomas can cause seizures in affected individuals.
● Memory Loss: Cognitive impairment and memory loss may occur.
● Changes in Personality: Behavioral changes and personality alterations.
● Weakness: Weakness in the arms or legs may be experienced.
● Vision Problems: Blurred or double vision may be a symptom.


Treatments

● Surgery: Surgical removal of the tumor if feasible.
● Radiation Therapy: Targeted radiation to kill cancer cells.
● Chemotherapy: Medications to destroy or slow down cancer cells.
● Immunotherapy: Boosting the body's immune system to fight cancer.

Meningioma


Symptoms

● Headaches: Common symptom, often severe.
● Vision Changes: Blurred or double vision.
● Memory Loss: Impaired memory and concentration.
● Seizures: Meningiomas can cause seizures.
● Weakness: Weakness in the limbs may occur.
● Personality Changes: Behavioral changes and mood swings.


Treatments

● Surgery: Removal of the meningioma through surgery.
● Radiation Therapy: Targeted radiation to eliminate remaining cancer cells.
● Medications: Sometimes prescribed to control symptoms.

Alzheimer's Disease


Symptoms

● Memory Loss: Progressive memory decline is a key symptom.
● Cognitive Decline: Impaired thinking and reasoning abilities.
● Difficulty Performing Familiar Tasks: Challenges in daily activities.
● Language Problems: Difficulty in communication and finding words.
● Disorientation: Confusion about time and place.
● Changes in Mood and Behavior: Mood swings and personality changes.


Treatments

● Medications: Donepezil, Rivastigmine, or Memantine may be prescribed.
● Cognitive Therapies: Strategies to improve memory and cognitive functions.
● Supportive Care: Providing assistance with daily activities.
● Clinical Trials: Participation in research studies for potential treatments.
